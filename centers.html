Ambedkar Vihar, Sector 37, Noida
(Centre & Head Office)


This centre commenced in 1999 with just 15 children. Since then, there has been no looking back. The growth has been phenomenal and the centre now caters to over 600 children through its various programmes.

This centre, caters to the slum at Harijan Basti, Ambedkar Vihar, Sector 37, Noida. The residents consist of landlords and tenants. The tenants are mostly migrants from various places in the country. The average monthly income of each household would be Rs 5000-6000 per month. The earning members are mostly domestic servants, vegetable vendors, rickshaw pullers, tailors, etc. This centre caters to over 350 children who attend our School Programme and over 250 children of our After School Support Programme (ASSP) and Foundation Programme. This is further supported by the Arts & Performing Arts Programme, Volunteer Programme and Life Skills Programme.

Vatika Centre, Sector 128, Noida Expressway
Shahpur Village, Sector 128 (Vatika), Noida


Vatika is located near the Noida - Greater Noida Expressway. Most of our children are from the nearby Shahpur Village, where the population consists mainly of uneducated farmers. Due to land acquisition by the government, these farmers have sold off their land at a very princely sum. With this their economic status has improved but the education level remains the same.

The Vatika Centre opened up in 2005 and presently caters to over 170 children.

Khoda Centre, Sector 62, Noida


Our third centre is at Khoda Village in Sector 62. It reaches out to children of the Khoda Village. This is a heavily populated area mainly with low income group families. There are also many migratory and casual labourers.
This centre caters to over 170 children who attend our School Programme. It has 9 classrooms and a library.

Opened in 2007 and presently caters to 160+ children

Arise Lab School Project (under the PPP model)

The work that we have done in communities for 16 years has been recognized and is now being replicated through one of its kind Public-Private Partnership (PPP) model between East Delhi Municipal Corporation, Tech Mahindra Foundation and Vidya & Child, wherein Vidya & Child has been actively engaged as a Technical Partner to replicate its programmes in a Government School in Delhi.
Vidya & Child is proud to be a part of this new paradigm.
It provides us with an opportunity to work under a PPP
model and endeavour to bring about a change in the
education scenario in India.
Under this project, we reach out to more than 225
children belonging to the underprivileged communities of
Dilshad Garden, Tahirpur, Nandnagri, Seemapuri etc. in
East Delhi.
A team comprising of specialized resources guided by a Core Project Team and a Dedicated Project Coordinator are jointly working with the existing team of the school to bring about an improvement in the overall quality of education and motivation of children, parents & teachers.
